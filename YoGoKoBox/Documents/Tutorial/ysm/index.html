YoGoKo Smart Middleware

    Overview

    YoGoKo’s communication stack is based on YoGoKo Y-Smart Middleware, a middleware and a framework dedicated to ITS.

    Y-Smart Middleware is built on the following concepts:

    • Microservices: Components (facilities, network, applications, etc) are implemented as microservices which implement a specific and independent function. Microservices may interact and communicate with each other and share common data.
    • Internal mechanisms: The middleware offers mechanisms to be used by internal and external services, making it easy and straightforward to exchange data between services. A pubsub (publish-subscribe) mechanism allows transmission of datagrams between services. A cache mechanism maintains an internal database that can be accessed by all services. A config mechanism allows dynamic control, configuration, and retrieval of service statistics.
    • Multi-APIs: APIs are provided for a lot of different usages, they provide access to middleware mechanisms. Programmatic APIs are provided for implementing services. Web-based APIs are provided for implementing HMIs. ROS and RTMaps APIs are provided for integrating in Connected and Autonomous Vehicle environments. APIs and connectors are designed to be iso-functional.
    • Multi-formats: Specific mechanisms and services may serve applications and other services with several formats (ASN.1 UPER, JSON, XML, Human-readable, etc), the formatting being automatically made by middleware mechanisms.

    In order to implement the full ETSI ITS stack, Y-Smart Middleware is extended by complementary YoGoKo components:

    • PKIService: implements communications with a C-ITS PKI
    • YoGoKo libgn: implements GN, GN6ASL, BTP and WAVE protocols, packaged as itsnet daemon or in the atlk-ygk ThreadX image

    IPv4/IPv6 protocols are implemented through the standard network layer provided by the Operating System.

    Supported standards

    The YoGoKo Y-Smart communication stack (including complementary YoGoKo components) supports the following standards:

    ITS Station reference architecture

    • ISO 21217 – 2014 : Architecture
    • ISO TS 17419 – v1.1.1 : ITS-AID register

    802.11p/ITS-G5 radio communications

    • ETSI EN 302 571 – v1.1.1 : Usage of 5.9GHz frequencies
    • ETSI EN 302 663 – v1.2.1 : G5 access layer
    • ETSI TS 102 724 – v1.1.1 : G5 specifications
    • ETSI TS 102 792 – v1.1.1 : CEN DSRC-G5 mitigation

    Network protocols

    • ISO 21210 – 2012 : IPv6 support for ITS
    • IETF RFC 2460 (and related) : IPv6 Protocol
    • ETSI EN 302 636-4-1 – v1.2.1 (2014-05): GN protocol
    • ETSI EN 302 636-4-1 – v1.3.1 (2017-08): GN protocol
    • ETSI EN 302 636-5-1 – v1.2.1 (2014-08): BTP protocol
    • ETSI EN 302 636-5-1 – v2.1.1 (2017-08): BTP protocol
    • ETSI EN 302 636-6-1 - v1.2.1 : GN6 protocol
    • IEEE P1606.3v3 : Wireless Access in Vehicular Environment (WAVE)

    V2X messaging and facilities

    • ETSI EN 302 637-2 – v1.3.2 (2014-11): Part 2: Specification of Cooperative Awareness Basic Service
    • ETSI EN 302 637-2 – v1.4.1 (2019-04): Part 2: Specification of Cooperative Awareness Basic Service
    • ETSI EN 302 637-3 – v1.2.2 (2014-11): Part 3: Specifications of Decentralized Environmental Notification Basic Service
    • ETSI EN 302 637-3 – v1.3.1 (2019-04): Part 3: Specifications of Decentralized Environmental Notification Basic Service
    • ETSI EN 302 931 – v1.1.1 (2011-07): Geographical Area Definition
    • ETSI TS 102 894-1 – v1.1.1 : Facilities layer
    • ETSI TS 102 894-2 – v1.2.1 (2014-09) : CDD dictionary
    • ETSI TS 102 894-2 – v1.3.1 (2018-08) : CDD dictionary
    • ISO/TS 19091:2016(E): Using V2I and I2V communications for applications related to signalized intersections
    • ISO/TS 19091:2018(E): Using V2I and I2V communications for applications related to signalized intersections
    • ISO 19321 : IVI
    • ETSI TS 103 301 V1.1.1 (2016-11): Facilities layer protocols and communication requirements for infrastructure services (SPATEM, MAPEM, IVIM, SREM, SSEM messaging)
    • ETSI TS 103 301 V1.2.1 (2018-08): Facilities layer protocols and communication requirements for infrastructure services (SPATEM, MAPEM, IVIM, SREM, SSEM messaging)
    • SAE J2735 2016: Dedicated Short Range Communications (DSRC) Message Set Dictionary
    • SAE J2945/1 2016: On-Board System Requirements for V2V Safety Communications
